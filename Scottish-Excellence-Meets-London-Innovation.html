Scottish Excellence Meets London Innovation

Weddings Spotlight

  • Schivas Steading in Ellon took home Wedding Venue of the Year, while The Dougarie Boathouse earned Outstanding Achievement recognition—proof that Scotland's countryside estates and unique settings continue to set the benchmark for unforgettable weddings.
  • Fife's strong showing lived on through finalists like Cambo Estate (Kingsbarns, a romantic/exclusive contender and co-ordinator star), Rufflets (St Andrews), and Balbirnie House (Glenrothes).
  • These venues embody the year's big shift: freer 2026 laws allowing ceremonies in beaches, gardens, woodlands, and truly personal spots.
  • Couples are leaning into intimate twilight weddings—shorter hires that free budgets for dramatic lighting, bespoke decor, and magic-hour moments—making these award-honored Scottish gems even more sought-after for 2026/2027 dates.

Corporate Events Highlights

  • Meanwhile, in London's City, a new chapter is unfolding for corporate events. 1 Basinghall Avenue from Cavendish Venues opened its doors this month as a purpose-built powerhouse: a large auditorium, flexible breakouts, premium AV, and space for up to 450 guests or 30 exhibition stands.
  • Designed for seamless conferences, product launches, and high-impact meetings, it's arriving just as trends demand immersive, experiential formats over traditional setups.
  • Other fresh openings add to the momentum: phased launches at 88 Wood Street (a collaboration-focused hub starting March) and the luxurious rooftop botanical escape at House of Gods in Canary Wharf
  • Planners are prioritizing sustainability, AI integration, hybrid capabilities, and bigger budgets—with lead times stretching 18–24 months for the best spaces.
